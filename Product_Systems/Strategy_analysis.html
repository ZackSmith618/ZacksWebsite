Executive Summary

        

Rise Above is a charity dedicated to providing homes for older homeless individuals in Bolivia. The organization currently has an unorganized online presence that makes it difficult to grow and adequately advertise events. This project will consist of making a website and database for them in order to consolidate their online presence and help them connect more consistently with potential volunteers and donors.

Current State: The long-term goal of the Rise Above is to raise enough money to fund the construction in Bolivia. The shorter-term objectives consist primarily of running fundraising events. Rise Above currently relies on a partner organization called Choice Humanitarian for their website, but they are unsatisfied with the quality and consistency of their online presence and require a custom-built website to meet their needs.

Future State:  We anticipate that this project will be completed in May 2020. At that point, they will have a consistent, high-quality website and be better equipped to run events and fundraise. This will include any necessary information pages, an event calendar, an online registration system for events, and a link to their donation page which will still be processed by Choice Humanitarian.

Change Strategy: In order to meet Rise Above’s needs, our team will be meeting with their leadership to determine exactly what they want for the database and website.  Our system will help in providing a website for Rise Above so that they can have a central location for doing their work and running things online.  Having an upcoming events and fundraisers tab or directly on the homepage to help users navigate what they want.  Information pages to help the user know what they want to know about Rise Above.  An event calendar to show upcoming events.  It will also include an online registry system for the events while also providing a link to their donation page that is processed by Choice Humanitarian.  Because their current online presence is fairly limited, we do not anticipate significant difficulty in transitioning to the new website.

Risks:  Most of the project is fairly low risk. The potential areas of concern are the security of the database, which will house confidential information, and the dependence on Choice Humanitarian to process transactions. Our team has determined that the risks do not outweigh the benefits of this project and we will be moving forward with it.

This website will consolidate Rise Above’s online presence into one useful hub and allow them to better promote their events and expeditions. This will ultimately result in better fundraising potential and allow them to grow as an organization and build more homes for people in need.

1.1 Current Organization Strategy

Mission:  To provide homes for the elderly homeless in Villa Serrano, Bolivia.

Vision: To aid in the development of rural villages throughout Bolivia. Rise Above strives to keep these core values: integrity, responsibility, respect, human equality, courage, and moral progress.

Goals: To raise money to fund expeditions to Bolivia and promote Rise Above to encourage individuals to participate.

Objectives: Host yearly fundraising events such as: Latin festival, gift wrapping at Barnes & Nobel, FUNDrive at Savers, yard sales, and photoshoots.

1.2 Business Problem/Need

Rise Above works with a third-party nonprofit organization called Choice Humanitarian to run and manage their website. However, Choice Humanitarian does not provide all things needed for Rise Above.

Problem: Choice Humanitarian does not provide an organized way to promote other non-profit groups partnered with them. Rise Above’s donation page is hard to find and difficult to navigate, it is difficult to know about events, there is no sign-up link provided for events and expeditions, and Rise Above’s information is scattered throughout Choice Humanitarian’s webpage and Facebook.

Solution: Create a website that is tailored towards Rise Above’s needs. Content would include: an event calendar, an expedition sign-up form, a fundraising link, an ‘About Us’ page, and past event/photos page. This website will improve credibility, increase traffic, and provide an organized way to manage events for both the organization and volunteers.

Expected Benefits:

  • Individuals will have an easier time locating the donations page
  • Volunteers will have the ability to plan events by viewing the calendar and signing up
  • Event managers will have an organized schedule for volunteers
  • Individuals will have the ability to learn about Rise Above through the ‘About Us’ page

Cost of Doing Nothing:

  • Less money received in donations
  • Less credibility towards Rise Above
  • Less volunteers signing up for events and expeditions
  • More labor required to manage events and expeditions.

3.2.1 Change Strategy

Our team will help Rise Above create their own website; with elements they wish to incorporate. Included will be an event calendar that will contain future and past events, an expedition sign-up form that will upload to a database, fundraising link and registration links, an ‘About Us’ page, and past event/photos page. To accomplish this proposed change, our team will include these elements within a website and database that will be Rise Above’s. Our team will meet twice a week, along with constant communication through Slack to make sure each deliverable of the project is being met.

3.2.2 Transition States and Release Planning

Transition States: Creating Rise Above’s website and database should only require a few transition states. Our team will first gather information that is wanted/needed on the new website and database. We will then transition over to creating mockups and completing initial creation and testing for bugs. The last transition will be Rise Above’s completed website and database.

Release Planning: in the initial phases of planning, the mission, values and requirements of Rise Above will be analyzed. Our team will meet with stakeholders and Rise Above’s leadership to determine what should be included within the website and database. Next, our team will design both the website and database, making sure that it meets requirements. We will then deliver mockups to Rise Above. From there, initial testing and creation will take place. During each phase of testing and releasing, our team will make sure that requirements and deliverables are being met.

3.3 Enterprise Readiness Assessment

Stakeholders have been met with and Rise Above is an organization that is ready to take charge of their own website and database. Rise Above is currently under Choice Humanitarian’s management and they have noticed that Choice is constantly changing their website, making it difficult for Rise Above to promote their organization. Through the creation of their own website, Rise Above will be able to promote fundraising, expeditions, and events with ease.

 4.1 Possible Negative Impact to Value (quantify)

Most of the changes our project team is going to make are of low risk as shown in the Risk Assessment Matrix table above. Changes to the advertising strategy of the organization through more efficient tool such as a new website. The possible negative impact is showed in the table related to collecting of some volunteers, expedition’s participants and donor’s information. Because this organization is going to collect only basic contact information such as: first & last name, address, email, phone number, the interests of volunteering and comments, the possible breach of security and lost or disclose of collected data are very unlikely. Because the collected data would be so limited and would not contain high level confidentiality info, though its lost would be unacceptable, even intolerable with the necessary level of security that  could be prevented. The most serious and extremely intolerable would be a security breach on its partner’s donation webpage to which donors will be redirected. Of course, a ny incidents that might result in unauthorized access of data, applications from the partner’s services, networks and/or devices by bypassing their underlying security would have a negative impact not only on Choice Humanitarian, a partner’s organization , but also on Rise Above.

Despite some level of risk, the value that the proposed changes will bring to the organization’s functionality, marketing strategy and progress by this project will exceed the possible risk, which also can be minimized by creating a higher level of database security on Rise Above as well as mitigation of improving the security level on the partner’s website.

4.2 Constraints, Assumptions, Dependencies

Unfortunately, because the Rise Above Bolivia is not a registered non-profit organization and doesn’t have its ENI number and status of exempt charity organization, it must rely on Choice Humanitarian as a partner at this time for donations and for organizing the expeditions and projects. With some assumptions this partnership can be considered a benefit to the Rise Above because the organization doesn’t need a lot of funding or resources to allocate to its  own administrative and servicing systems. Through at the same time it can add its resources to the established and well-organized partner who can pull out larger expeditions and projects.

4.3 Unknowns

There are some unknowns in this project. For example, though we can assume that creating a new website will improve the advertising ability of the Rise Above organization, we will not know for sure how much it will actually increase the number of participants, donors and donations. We also know that building of database will improve the process, functionality,  and quality of service of this organization in the near future, we don’t know for a fact how often built by our team  database will be continually updated and who and how will maintain it due to the limited abilities and skills of the  current Rise Above volunteers. We hope that some of our team members will contribute their time as new volunteers and that will definitely help to maintain the newly built database. We know that it will be a great tool in the hands of experienced people, but we don’t know whether the organization will find those volunteers to run & sustain the database function or not without our help.

4.4 Risk Tolerance

The continual negotiation with the partner Choice Humanitarian and in mitigation of extra reliable security protection for the donation database will increase the success of both organizations and prevent downfalls.

4.5 Recommendation

(pursue, pursue while mitigating risk, increase benefits to outweigh risks, do not pursue)

Based on the Risk Assessment Matrix, the project team decided to pursue the proposed changes to the organizational service structure and advertising methods by building a new website & creating a new database.

4.6 Mitigating Strategies

This is how our team suggests preventing risks and implementing mitigating strategies to prevent/reduce impact of likelihood of risks related to this project. Our team will recommend a few ways to increase the security level of donation website and protection of database:
